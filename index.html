Oscilloscape

Rhythmic Intelligence for Human Interaction

Recent breakthroughs in neuroscience may enable solutions to previously intractable problems in diverse areas from digital health to artificial intelligence. Oscilloscape is an early stage company creating human-centered technologies based on groundbreaking research in the neuroscience of rhythm. Our core mission is to develop effective digital therapies to build language fluency in aphasia and dyslexia, and to rehabilitate memory and cognition in Alzheimer's disease.

RHYTHMHas Power
Human-centered technologies based on groundbreaking research in the neuroscience of rhythm

Recent research shows that rhythm is key to unlocking the mysteries of the human brain. Oscilloscape is developing solutions based on groundbreaking discoveries that reveal how brain rhythms synchronize our behaviors with events and people in the world around us. Our technology simulates the way the brain synchronizes to sounds and coordinates rhythmic behaviors like speech, music, and movement. We use these capabilities to develop digital and device-based therapies to rehabilitate patients who suffer from language, cognitive, and motor impairments.
Our rhythmic neural networks have other uses as well. Clarity listening technologies show the promise of improving speech clarity in noisy, real-world environments to enhance the performance of hearing aids and intelligent virtual assistants. Synchrony offers real-time perceptual analysis of music, with diverse applications from home lighting to augmented reality.

HARNESSING THE POWER OF BRAIN RHYTHMS


Our patented technology mimics neural synchronization and neuroplasticity, enabling us to create advanced simulations of rhythmic activity and learning in the human brain. When auditory and motor rhythms are stimulated simultaneously, the brain forms connections between auditory and motor areas that support coordinated behaviors and human interactions. These principles underlie our approach to digital health, and form the basis for disruptive signal processing and pattern learning techniques.
